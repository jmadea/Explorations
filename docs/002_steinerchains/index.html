Exploration 002: Steiner Chains

In this Exploration, we're going to play with Steiner chains.

As the Wikipedia article explains, a Steiner chain is a set of circles that are tangent to two non-intersecting circles and where each circle in the chain is tangent to its neighbors. There are many arrangements of circles that meet these requirements. We're going begin our Exploration with the simplest of Steiner chains: annular Steiner chains. These are closed chains, which means that the first and last circle in the chain are tangent to each other. Additionally, the circles in annular Steiner chains are of equal size. Annular chains are tangent to inner and outer concentric circles.
